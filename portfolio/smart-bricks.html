Problem Statement ✦

Real estate investors face significant challenges in accessing reliable and comprehensive data to make informed investment decisions.

Investors struggle to find reliable, data-driven insights on real estate investments, making it challenging to assess ROI and identify optimal investment opportunities.

Traditional methods of evaluating properties are time-consuming and often lack predictive analytics, making it difficult to accurately forecast returns on investment.

Target audience ✦

Individuals:

  • People looking to invest in real estate properties, ranging from first-time investors to experienced professionals.
  • High Net Worth Individuals (HNWIs), Wealthy individuals seeking lucrative investment opportunities.
  • Property Owners, Individuals or entities owning finished properties who want to collaborate on selling or renting their properties.
Solution✦

Develop a smart platform that utilizes AI to provide comprehensive real estate investment analytics, enabling users to simulate ROI, discover investment opportunities, and make informed decisions based on city-specific market data.

Smart Bricks leverages AI to provide real estate investors with detailed analytics and ROI simulations, enabling them to make data-driven investment decisions.

The platform offers an intuitive interface to explore top cities, analyze market trends, and access project-specific insights, streamlining the investment process.

__Search Initiation Flow
What problem does this feature solve?

Smart Bricks hosts an extensive database of real estate listings, creating a need for a search feature that narrows down options to match users' investment preferences effectively.

Without an optimized search flow, users could become overwhelmed, leading to high bounce rates and disengagement. 

Why is this feature necessary?

Investing in real estate involves numerous variables and personal preferences, making it a complex decision-making process.

This feature simplifies the process by gathering key data to provide curated, relevant listings, enhancing user experience and decision-making.

Goals
  • Simplify the search flow to reduce overwhelm.
  • Deliver curated listings based on user preferences.
  • Provide search criteria that help users understand the market and regional dynamics better.
  • Keep users involved throughout the search process, ensuring they remain engaged even beyond the MVP.
Success Metrics:

Quantitative:

  • Bounce Rate & U-turn Rate.
  • Exit Percentage & Conversion Rates.

Qualitative:

  • User feedback on the ease and engagement of the onboarding and search flow, ensuring minimal friction.
 Challenge - [Keyword Search Limitation] 

Due to technical constraints with API integrations from various property vendors, the MVP-1 phase could not support a robust keyword search functionality.

Iterative Development

Planned future integration of keyword search functionality as API issues are resolved post-MVP.

Overcoming the Challenge

After thorough research and collaboration with the backend team, we identified that search criteria could be refined through pre-defined search lists.

This approach bypassed the immediate need for keyword search by categorizing properties based on critical investment factors (e.g., location, property type, projected ROI).

This allowed us to deliver a functional and user-friendly search experience within the MVP-1 constraints.

__Impact
Quantitative
  1. Achieved a 25% decrease in bounce rates from users engaging with the new search flow.
  2. Improved conversion rates by 15% for users completing the search process, indicating greater user satisfaction and engagement.
Qualitative
  1. Positive user feedback emphasized the clarity and usefulness of the sorting feature, particularly for ROI and rental yield comparisons.
  2. Users found the pre-defined search lists intuitive, which significantly improved their overall experience and satisfaction with the search process.

__Watchlist & Shortlist Flows
What problem does this feature solve?

Users often have multiple investment priorities, requiring an efficient way to organize and monitor various search results.

The Watchlists feature addresses this need by allowing users to create multiple lists tailored to different investment preferences.

The auto-pilot mode further enhances this by automatically updating the watchlists and notifying users of new matching opportunities.

Why is this feature necessary?

Given the vast number of property listings, users need tools to streamline their search process.

The Watchlists feature allows them to manage different investment interests without manual tracking, making it easier to stay updated on new opportunities that match their criteria.

For instance, a user may simultaneously track off-plan investments and ready-to-move properties, each requiring distinct parameters.

Goals
  • Allow users to create and manage multiple Watchlists based on varying investment preferences.
  • Provide an auto-pilot mode to automatically refresh watchlists and notify users of new matching properties.
  • Keep users actively engaged by sending timely notifications of relevant investment opportunities.
Success Metrics:

Quantitative:

  • Measure the click-through rate of notifications sent for new matching opportunities.
  • Track the number of active watchlists per user and how frequently they are accessed or modified.

Qualitative:

  • Gather user feedback to assess the clarity and usefulness of notifications and the ease of managing multiple watchlists.
 Challenge 

Risk of cluttering the Watchlist Cards with too many details, making them difficult to manage.

Ensuring essential details are not overlooked, providing users with all they need at a glance.

Overcoming the Challenge

By focusing on a clear information hierarchy, we emphasized key metrics (like property type, location, and ROI) while using concise summaries for less critical data.

We also optimized the auto-pilot mode to deliver timely, relevant notifications without overwhelming the user.

__Impact
Quantitative
  1. A 30% increase in engagement from users clicking on notifications related to new property matches.
  2. A 25% rise in the number of watchlists created and actively managed by users.
Qualitative
  1. Users reported high satisfaction with the auto-pilot mode, appreciating the automated updates and reduced manual effort in managing their investments.
  2. Feedback highlighted the ease of switching between multiple watchlists, making it simple to track different investment opportunities simultaneously.
